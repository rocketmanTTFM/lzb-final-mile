Program Overview

What we're building — and what we're looking for

La-Z-Boy Residential is standardizing final-mile delivery across a 36-market Z-Dock network. Rocket Shipping is managing carrier selection, performance measurement, and rate administration on La-Z-Boy's behalf. This page is a summary of the program and the bid we're bringing to market.

1

Scope

White-glove residential home delivery from each Z-Dock direct to consumer. Any stop within 150 driving miles of the Z-Dock, 2-man team, in-home placement, basic assembly, debris removal. All 36 markets in one program, market-by-market award.

2

Partner we're seeking

Reliable, reputable regional and national carriers with home-delivery experience in big-&-bulky or furniture. 2-man teams, background-checked drivers, branded or uniformed crews, TMS/tracking integration, and a history of hitting 95%+ on-time in their current book.

3

Commitment from La-Z-Boy

Firm All-In Truck Rates for 12 months. Dedicated capacity guarantees available for carriers who commit. Weekly performance reporting and feedback, transparent DFS fuel mechanism, accessorial pay on every qualifying event. Phased rollout aligned to Z-Dock launch schedule.

Rate Structure · Carrier-Facing Program Rules

One All-In Truck Rate. A smarter fuel supplement. Drive more, earn more.

La-Z-Boy has engineered a carrier-friendly rate structure designed to remove rate creep, protect carriers against diesel volatility, and reward long-mile days with fair compensation — not penalize them.

01

All-In Truck Rate

A single all-in daily rate per truck covering any stop within 150 one-way driving miles of the Z-Dock. One number per market. No zones, no tiers, no per-stop or per-mile surcharges.

  • Dispatch + 2-man white-glove team
  • Standard equipment (26' box / liftgate)
  • In-home placement, basic assembly, debris removal
  • All stops within the day's manifest
  • Firm for 12 months from go-live
  • Fuel handled separately via the DFS →
02

Distance & Fuel Supplement (DFS)

A mileage-based supplement that pays carriers more on long-mile days — indexed weekly to DOE diesel. Replaces the traditional percentage-of-rate FSC. Directly aligns compensation with actual fuel and wear.

DFS / mile = ( DOE weekly diesel ÷ 8 MPG ) × 2.0

Service Level Requirements

Performance carriers commit to — weekly measurement

Each awarded market is measured weekly in a Rocket-operated performance portal. All awarded carriers receive their own dashboard. Lane-level performance is reviewed monthly. Below-target performance triggers a 30-day corrective action plan; two consecutive months below target market-wide triggers volume reallocation.

Accessorials

Carrier-proposed schedule

Carriers are asked to submit their own accessorial schedule with the bid — cataloging the events your operation charges for (stairs, long carry, haul-away, wait time, reschedules, weekend delivery, and any others specific to your market), along with your proposed rates and units of measure. La-Z-Boy and Rocket review the schedule during bid evaluation and confirm alignment at award. Accessorials are paid separately from the All-In Truck Rate and the DFS, and are monitored by Rocket for utilization and outliers once you're awarded.

Timeline & Process

From RFP release to first dispatch

The RFP process is short and deliberate. Two and a half weeks from release to bids due. All dates below are working targets pending RFP release.

STEP 1
RFP Release

April 20, 2026 — Full RFP, pricing workbook, and qualification questionnaire issued to the invited carrier pool. Markets, service levels, and rate rules documented in detail.

STEP 2
Q&A Window

Apr 20 – Wed, Apr 29, 2026. Written questions via email. All answers distributed to all bidders equally so every carrier bids on the same information.

STEP 3
Bids Due

Fri, May 8, 2026 · 5:00 PM CT. Submit all bids (pricing workbook + qualification materials + carrier-proposed accessorial schedule) to gabe@gorocketshipping.com. Any subset of the 36 markets may be bid.

STEP 4
Bid Review

Rocket reviews every submission with La-Z-Boy using a standardized scoring model (pricing, capability, track record, fit). Finalist interviews held as needed.

STEP 5
Award & Contracting

Rocket issues market-by-market award notifications and executes the carrier contracts. Rates firm for 12 months from go-live.

STEP 6
Operational Onboarding

La-Z-Boy handles operational onboarding of awarded carriers after Rocket completes award and contracting in Step 5 — Z-Dock introductions, appointment-window alignment, first-dispatch coordination.
